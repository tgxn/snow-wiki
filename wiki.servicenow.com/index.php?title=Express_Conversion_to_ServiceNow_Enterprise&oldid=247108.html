Express Conversion to ServiceNow Enterprise

1 Overview

When a ServiceNow Express instance is converted to the Enterprise Service Management Suite, the instance is changed significantly. To minimize the impact on day-to-day operation, the conversion is designed so that ITIL and ESS users do not notice changes in how they use it. The administrator is given the authority required to control the instance functionality and access.

The administrator is asked not to make any changes to the converted instance until the process is completed. Making changes during the process can cause significant issues and delays for the conversion.

2 Conversion Process

The conversion process includes an opportunity to review and test the Enterprise Service Management Suite with a copy of your data before the production instance is converted. It follows this timeline:

  1. A sub-production instance is created from a cloned copy of your ServiceNow Express production instance. This sub-production instance is for the full Enterprise Service Management Suite.
  2. You receive a letter indicating that you are entering a 15-day testing phase. The purpose is to verify that your ITIL and self-service portal users continue to experience the behavior they are familiar with.
  3. After you verify with the sub-production instance that the conversion completed and is acceptable, your designated HI administrator submits an incident on HI to advise ServiceNow of your acceptance.
  4. ServiceNow schedules a full conversion of your Express production instance to the Enterprise Service Management Suite. Your sub-production instance is paired to it, and is available for you to configure new features, validate them, and roll them out to production.

3 ESS and ITIL Experience

After an Express instance converts to the Enterprise Service Management Suite, users with ESS and ITIL roles experience the look, feel, and behavior they are familiar with. They can access the same sets of applications, modules, lists, and forms, and they experience the same underlying behavior. All functions that were accessible to ESS and ITIL users in Express look and behave the same way for all users after the conversion.

For example:

  • The Incident form looks and works exactly the same way as it did in the Express instance. The same is true for the Problem, Change Request, and Configuration Item forms.
  • Approvals are requested the same way as in the Express instance, and the approval process is the same.
  • Survey responses are generated the same way as in the Express instance.

4 Administrator Experience

The Enterprise Service Management Suite is significantly different for administrators, who have access to all of its features. It is recommended that administrators attend the ServiceNow System Administration training.

4.1 Plugins

There are some differences in activated plugins between an Enterprise instance converted from Express and a new Enterprise instance. The converted instance has fewer activated plugins.

Functionally, there are two types of ServiceNow plugins: platform layer plugins and application layer plugins. Platform layer plugins are those that all ServiceNow users can benefit from and use, such as the visual task boards plugin. Application layer plugins are useful for some customers but not necessarily all customers.

New application layer plugins are included with every release, and they are typically activated on new Enterprise instances. But they are not activated automatically for upgraded instances. The Express to Enterprise conversion process follows the same strategy as a typical release upgrade, such as Eureka to Fuji. During an Express conversion to Enterprise, platform layer plugins are activated, and application layer plugins are activated except for those that are not activated during an upgrade.

For example, the Content Management (CMS) plugin is activated with new Enterprise instances. But because it is add-on functionality, the ServiceNow plugin support team determined not to activate it automatically for an instance that is being upgraded from Berlin, that did not offer this functionality, to Fuji. Therefore, the Content Management plugin is not activated automatically during an Express to Enterprise conversion. After the conversion, administrators can decide which application layer plugins they need, and activate the ones they select.

After the conversion, the administrator navigates to System Definition > Plugins to see the list of plugins that are available and active. The administrator can go to the List of Plugins on the wiki to learn about the active plugins and to see other plugins that are available or that can be requested.

4.2 Scripting

Administrators have access to core ServiceNow platform features such as scripted business rules, client scripts, UI actions, UI pages, UI macros, UI scripts, script includes, script actions, and so on. Service Management administrators have access to all tables and dictionary entries, and can use scripting to make changes in the instance.

4.3 Security

The Express platform uses simple security for create, read, update, delete (CRUD) access through the dictionary. The Enterprise Service Management Suite uses contextual security for more robust access control, referred to as access control lists (ACLs) or access control rules. During the conversion, your CRUD settings are converted to ACLs. The conversion does not change any of your security access.

Going forward, you will manage security settings using ACLs. For more information, see Access Control Rules and Security Best Practices.

4.4 Important Service Management Functionality

Explore the features listed below to begin taking advantage of the Enterprise Service Management Suite. Several of them require that you activate a plugin.

  • Update sets
  • SLAs
  • Workflow
  • Project management
  • SDLC scrum process
  • Import sets
  • Transform maps
  • LDAP
  • Content management
