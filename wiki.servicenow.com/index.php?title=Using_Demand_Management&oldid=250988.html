1 Overview

You can create, view, and modify demands using the Demand Management application if you have the demand manager role. You can also approve demands and create the following artifacts from the approved demands:

  • Project
  • Change
  • Enhancement
  • Defect

The type of artifact created from a demand depends on the selections in the Category and Type fields on the Demand form. Enhancements and defects can be created if the system administrator has activated the SDLC-SCRUM plugin. This feature is available starting with the Fuji release.

You can create and view demands using the Demand Management application if you have the demand user role.

2 Demand Management Life Cycle

The demand management life cycle can be simplified as follows:

  • Creating a demand:
    • The user submits an idea and the demand manager approves the idea, automatically creating a demand from that idea (Fuji release).
    • The user submits a demand (Eureka release).
  • Viewing a list of demands:
    • The demand manager views demands on the demand workbench or from a list view (Fuji release).
    • The demand manager views demands in a list view (Eureka release).
  • Enhancing a demand: The demand manager can send the demand to screening, which sends assessments to stakeholders.
  • Assessing a demand:
    • The demand manager can screen the demand and send surveys to stakeholders to complete assessments.
    • The demand manager can set the state of the demand to qualify, defer, or incomplete.
    • Demands can be analyzed and approved using the demand workbench.
  • Creating an artifact: the demand manager creates a project, enhancement, change or defect.

2.1 Demand States

In demand management, a demand can be in any of the following states.

Demand State Changes

2.1.1 Demand States for Fuji

The demand management application uses the following simplified demand states starting with the Fuji release.

State Description
Draft A submitted idea has been accepted by the demand manager.

After reviewing or editing the record, click one of these buttons:

  • Update: The demand record is updated, but the demand remains in the current state.
  • Submit demand: The demand is moved to the submitted state.
  • Delete: The demand record is deleted.
Submitted An accepted idea creates a demand record and the demand manager submits the demand.

After reviewing or editing the record, click one of these buttons:

  • Update: The demand record is updated, but the demand remains in the current state.
  • Screen: The demand is moved to the screening state.
  • Qualify: The demand is moved to the qualified state.
  • Defer: The demand is moved to the deferred state.
  • Incomplete: The demand is moved to the incomplete state.
  • Delete: The demand record is deleted.
Screening The demand manager initiates assessments for the demand.

After reviewing or editing the record, click one of these buttons:

  • Update: The demand record is updated, but the demand remains in the current state.
  • Qualify: The demand is moved to the qualified state.
  • Defer: The demand is moved to the deferred state.
  • Delete: The demand record is deleted.
Qualified The demand has been qualified and is ready for review.

After reviewing or editing the record, click one of these buttons:

  • Update: The demand record is updated, but the demand remains in the current state.
  • Approve: The demand is moved to the approved state.
  • Defer: The demand is moved to the deferred state.
  • Delete: The demand record is deleted.
Approved The demand is approved.

After reviewing or editing the record, click one of these buttons:

  • Update: The demand record is updated, but the demand remains in the current state.
  • Close: The demand is moved to the closed state.
  • Delete: The demand record is deleted.
Completed The demand is moved to the completed state.

These states appear in the process flow indicator at the top of the Demand form. The process flow indicator:

  • Highlights the current state of the demand.
  • Checks off the states that a demand has passed through.
  • Leaves blank the states that have been skipped.

In this example, the demand is currently in the Approved state. It passed through the Draft, Submitted, and Qualified states but skipped the Screening state.


2.1.2 Demand States for Eureka

The demand management application uses the demand states described in the following table for the Eureka release.

State Description
Pending The demand appears in the pending state only when it is created using the Demand Management application. Demands created through the service catalog start in the submitted state, not the pending state.

After reviewing or editing the record, click one of these buttons:

  • Update: The demand record is updated, but the demand remains in the current state.
  • Submit: The demand is moved to the submitted state.
  • Delete: The demand record is deleted.
Submitted The demand has been submitted and is now ready to be reviewed by the demand manager or demand user.

After reviewing or editing the record, click one of these buttons:

  • Update: The demand record is updated, but the demand remains in the current state.
  • Incomplete: The demand is moved to the incomplete state.
  • Screening: The demand is moved to the screening state.
Incomplete The demand is incomplete and requires additional information.

After reviewing or editing the record, click one of these buttons:

  • Update: The demand record is updated, but the demand remains in the current state.
  • Submit: The demand is moved to the submitted state.
  • Delete: The demand record is deleted.
Screening The demand has been reviewed for accuracy and completeness. Now, you can enhance the demand by adding risks, requirements, tasks, and stakeholders. If assessments have been configured, you can send assessments to assessment recipients. Similarly, if resource management is activated, you can create and add resource plans to the demand.

After reviewing or editing the record, click one of these buttons:

  • Update: The demand record is updated, but the demand remains in the current state.
  • Qualify: The demand is moved to the qualified state.
Qualified The demand has been populated with additional information, assessment results have been calculated, scorecards are available, and all form fields are set to read-only. Now, decision makers can view all of the information to make a decision.

After reviewing the record, click one of these buttons:

  • Approve: The demand is moved to the approved state.
  • Deferred: The demand is moved to the deferred state.
  • Reject: The demand is moved to the rejected state.
Approved The demand record is locked. However, you can still modify some of the related list items.

After modifying a related list item or after the task the demand refers to is complete, click one of these buttons:

  • Update: The demand record is updated, but the demand remains in the current state.
  • Completed: The demand is moved to the completed state.
  • Reject: The demand is moved to the rejected state.
Deferred You can modify all form fields and some of the related list items.

After you edit the record, click one of these buttons:

  • Update: The demand record is updated, but the demand remains in the current state.
  • Approve: The demand is moved to the approved state.
  • Reject: The demand is moved to the rejected state.
Rejected The demand record is locked. However, you can still modify some of the related list items.
Completed The demand record is locked. However, you can still modify some of the related list items.

3 Viewing Demands

The demand manager can view demands by navigating to one of the following:

  • Demand > Demands > Workbench: (Fuji release) The bubble chart and the list view on the demand workbench display all qualified demands.
  • Demand > Demands > All: (Fuji release) Displays the Demands list.
  • Demand Management > Demands > All: (Eureka release) Displays the Demands list.

The demand user can view demands by navigating to one of the following:

  • Demand > Demands > All: (Fuji release) Displays the Demands list.
  • Demand Management > Demands > All: (Eureka release) Displays the Demands list.

The Demand field on the Demands list is a composite field that combines the demand name and number, starting with the Fuji release. The demand number provides a link to the Demand form.

The Stage field displays the status or progress of a demand and is updated as the demand moves through the life cycle, from an idea to a demand to the resulting project, enhancement, change, or defect. At a glance, users can use this field to track the progress of a demand. For more information, see Stage Fields.

Pointing to the Stage field on the Demands list displays a pop-up window that shows the progression of the idea along with the value of the State field for each step in the progression:

  • Idea: If an idea is being evaluated, the pop-up window displays the current state of the idea.
  • Demand: if an idea is promoted to a demand, the pop-up window displays the current state of the demand as well as the idea.
  • Project, Enhancement, Change, or Defect: if a demand is accepted, the pop-up window displays the current state of the resulting artifact in addition to the demand and idea.
Idea Stage Summary pop-up window

 Note: Users cannot search or sort the Stage column on the Demands list.


The Project field displays the project name after the demand manager creates a project from the demand.

The Risk, Value, and Size fields display assessment metrics for the demand. This information appears on the Demands list, starting with the Fuji release.

4 Creating Demands

The demand manager can create a demand from the demand workbench. Demands created from the workbench are created in a qualified state.

The demand manager and the demand user can create a demand from the Demand application:

  1. Navigate to one of the following:
    • Demand > Demands > Create New (Fuji release).
    • Demand Management > Demands > Create New (Eureka release).
  2. Fill in the fields on the demand form as appropriate. See table for details.
    The fields on the form vary depending on the selections in the Category and Type fields.
  3. Right-click the form header and select Save to save the record.
  4. Enhance the demand by adding stakeholders, requirements, risks, assessment results, decisions, and resource plans, if needed.

5 Enhancing Demands

The demand manager typically works with a business relationship manager to identify stakeholders and elicit requirements, risks, and other important information. The Demand Management application streamlines the stakeholder identification process by auto-populating the list of stakeholders based on the portfolio the user selects when filling out the demand form in the service catalog or in the Demand Management application. The Demand Management application also allows you to use assessments to automate some of the information gathering process.

5.1 Adding Stakeholders

When a demand is submitted, the demand stakeholder list is populated automatically from the associated portfolio. You can also add additional stakeholders. To add a person to the stakeholder list, scroll to the Stakeholders related list on the Demand form, click New to create a record, and fill in the form as described in Populating the Stakeholder Registry. When adding a new stakeholder from the demand form, the Portfolio field displays the portfolio that was selected on the demand form.

A demand can have multiple stakeholders and a stakeholder can be associated with multiple demands.

Stakeholder Register form

The newly added stakeholder is automatically associated with the current demand and any other demands going forward that use the same portfolio.

5.2 Adding Requirements

Demand managers can create as many requirements as needed, but requirements are not mandatory. Typically, the stakeholders associated with a demand request should have input into what the requirements are for a demand request to be completed.

Demand managers can use the Requirement form to describe the requirement and assign an owner who is responsible for making sure the requirement is met. If a requirement is especially complex, demand managers can associate planned tasks, such as project tasks, with the requirement, and assign and track those tasks until they are complete.

To add a requirement to a demand, open a Demand form and click New on the Requirements related list (see table for field descriptions).

Requirements form

Field Description
Number Unique identification number for the requirement. This number is automatically generated when a new requirement record is created. Leaving this field blank or including spaces in the number results in an error message.
Source User requesting the requirement. This field is automatically populated with the name of the person filling out the form, but the source could be a different person who identified the requirement.
Owner User or group who is responsible for managing the requirement or making sure the requirement is met.
Estimated effort Approximate amount of time to complete the requirement and any associated tasks.
Priority Importance of the requirement as it applies to the overall demand.
State Status of the requirement can be Pending, Approved, or Rejected. Note that the request state is independent of the demand request state and of the states of any planned tasks associated with the requirement. The requirement state does not have to be closed to close the request. Likewise, closing the request does not change the state of the requirement.
Type Type of requirement can be: Business, Solution (Functional), Solution (Non-Functional), Quality, Stakeholder, or Transition.
Short description Brief description of the requirement.
Description Detailed description of the requirement and any associated tasks. For example, you can use this field to describe an expected outcome or result.

5.3 Adding Risks

A risk is any uncertain event that can potentially impact the success or outcome of a project. For example, schedule slippage is a risk, even if it's caused by incorrect estimating. Another example of a risk is a change in project requirements. It is important to record risks so decision makers have all relevant information when assessing a demand.

To add a risk to the demand, scroll to the Risks related list on the Demand form and click New to create a record (see table for field descriptions).

Risk form

Field Description
State Unique number for the requirement. This number is automatically generated when a new risk record is created.
Probability The likelihood that the event described by the risk will occur during the course of the project.
Short description [Required] Brief description of the event and its potential impact on the success of the activity specified in the demand.
Estimated cost Cost the event will generate should it occur.
Actual cost Cost generated by the event. This information is added when and if the event occurs.
Mitigation plan Brief description of efforts taken to mitigate the risk.

5.4 Recording Decisions

To add a decision to the demand, scroll to the Decisions related list on the Demand form and click New to create a record (see table for field descriptions).

Decision form

Field Description
Number Unique number for the decision. This number is automatically generated when a new decision record is created. Leaving this field blank or including spaces in the number results in an error message.
Requestor Person requesting the decision. This field is automatically populated with the name of the person entering the decision.
Due date The date the decision needs to be finalized.
Short description Brief description of the decision such as, what the decision is about, who made it, who it affects, and the decision outcome.
State The current state of the decision. Available options are: Pending, Approved, and Rejected.
Approval required Check box that enables (selected) or disables (cleared) the approval requirement for the decision.

5.5 Adding Resource Plans

Resource plans are another source of information that decision makers can use when evaluating demands. You can create resource plans for a demand from the Resource Plan related list, which appears only if the Resource Management application is activated.

To add a resource plans to the demand, scroll to the Resource Plan related list on the Demand form and click New to create a record. For more information, see creating resource plans.

6 Assessing Demands

6.1 Fuji Release

The Demand Management application comes with two demand visualization tools: the demand workbench and the demand roadmap. The demand workbench provides a single point of engagement for assessing and approving demands and creating projects, enhancements, changes, or defects. This page combines multiple views of demand information, including an interactive bubble chart and a detail area that displays the list of current demands. The demand roadmap is a visual representation of an organization's demands over time.

6.1.1 Using the Demand Workbench

A bubble chart is a graph that plots multiple demands based on three categories: risk, value, and size. Each demand is represented on the bubble chart by a circle which varies in size and color depending on the average of the scores for these categories. The bubble chart in the demand workbench displays all qualified demands and is dynamically updated as demands are created and assessed. This makes it a useful tool for demand managers, stakeholders, and decision makers to visually assess and compare demands. For more information, see Demand Workbench.

The list view on the demand workbench displays a list of the qualified demands that appear in the bubble chart. Selecting a demand from this list highlights the demand in the bubble chart and displays the demand form. The list view is also integrated with Live Feed so users can see current activity for a demand.

To access the demand workbench, navigate to Demand > Demands > Workbench.

6.1.2 Using the Roadmap

The roadmap is an interactive visualization tool that shows all demands that are currently in an active state. You can modify the look of the backlog using the Settings pane. The Settings pane allows you to change between the two-dimensional (2D) and three-dimensional (3D) view, filter demands by portfolio, or open the demands in a list view. While in list view, you can reassign panel colors, create filters to limit the records that are used for lanes and panels, and apply sorting. To use the roadmap, navigate to Demand > Roadmap. For more information, see Using Timeline Visualizations.

6.2 Eureka Release

The Demand Management application comes with the demand backlog and demand bubble chart, two tools that can aid decision makers with demand assessment. The demand backlog is a visual representation of an organization's demands over time, whereas the demand bubble chart provides a visual comparison of demands based on one or more sets of metrics that are specified in the bubble chart definition. Users with the demand_manager role can view backlog and bubble charts.

6.2.1 Using the Demand Backlog

The Demand Backlog is an interactive visualization tool that shows all demands that are currently in an active state. You can modify the look of the backlog using the Settings pane. The Settings pane allows you to change between the two-dimensional (2D) and three-dimensional (3D) view, filter demands by portfolio, or open the demands in a list view. While in list view, you can reassign panel colors, create filters to limit the records that are used for lanes and panels, and apply sorting.

To view the Demand Backlog, navigate to Demand Management > Visualization > Demand Backlog. For more information, see Using Timeline Visualizations.

6.2.2 Using the Demand Bubble Chart

A bubble chart is a graph that plots results for multiple demands. Bubble charts are dynamically updated as demands are created and assessed. After demand managers create a bubble chart, stakeholders and decision makers can use it to compare demands' relative standing.

To view the demand bubble chart, navigate to Demand Management > Bubble Charts > View.

Demand bubble chart

The bubble chart consists of both the chart and a list of the demands plotted. Each bubble chart shows data for three categories, making it easy to compare demands by category. The X- and Y-axes each represent a different category. Demands are plotted on the chart as circular markers, or bubbles, which vary in size according to scores for the third category, or Z-axis. Each bubble on the chart represents an average of category scores for a demand.

Point to a bubble to view a demand score summary. The summary displays the demand's average score for each category in the following order: X-axis category, Y-axis category, Z-axis category. The scores are based on data from a trailing twelve month period. Click a bubble or bubble label to view the demand record.

7 Qualifying Demands

When a demand is qualified, the assessment values are copied to the demand record and become the demand score values:

  • Size: the assessment score size
  • ROI: the assessment score value
  • Risk: the assessment score risk
  • Strategic alignment: the assessment score strategic alignment
  • Cost: the assessment score cost

These values can be updated from the demand workbench bubble chart or from the demand form.

8 Deleting Demands

Demands can be deleted only while in the Pending state. When you delete a demand, all data related to the demand, such as risks, requirements, and decisions are deleted. Stakeholders are deleted from the related list, but not from the Stakeholder Register [dmn_stakeholders_register] table. Only demand managers and demand users can delete demands.

To delete a demand:

  1. Navigate to Demand > Demands > All (Fuji) or Demand Management > Demands > All (Eureka).
  2. Do one of the following:
    • Click on the demand to open the demand form and then click Delete.
    • Select the check box next to the demand and then select Delete from the Actions choice list.

9 Creating an Artifact from a Demand

When you create a demand, you specify a category and a type for that demand. The selections from the Category and Type fields determine the artifact that can be created from a demand: project, enhancement, change, or defect. The Project Portfolio Suite plugin must be activated to create an enhancement or a defect.

  1. Navigate to Demand > Demands > Create New.
  2. Enter a unique Name for the demand.
  3. Select a Category.
  4. Select a Type.
  5. If desired, select a Portfolio.
  6. Click Submit to save the record.
  7. Click the related link to create the artifact.
    The related link appears as one of the following:
    • Create Project
    • Create Enhancement
    • Create Defect
    • Create Change
    If a project is created, the Project field is added to the Demand form and populated with a unique, auto-generated project number.
