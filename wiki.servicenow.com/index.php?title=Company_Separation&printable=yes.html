1 Introduction

 Note: Customers who need data separation or delegated administration should consider Domain separation instead of Company Separation. However, if company separation is already active when you activate domain separation, both plugins are active at the same time. To disable company separation, go to System Properties [sys_properties] table and delete the glide.db.separation.field and glide.db.separation.exception.field properties.


When Company Separation is activated on a ServiceNow instance, users with a Company value in their user record can only see data for their company and any child companies. Company Separation applies to any table that has a company or u_company field. For example, the task table has a company field. So a user in Company A will only be able to see tasks (incidents, change requests, problems, etc.) that are also assigned to Company A or Company A's hierarchical children.

To make Company Separation apply to a table that doesn't have a company field out-of-the-box, simply create a custom Company field on that table. Users without a company on their user record are not restricted by Company Separation.

2 Enabling

To activate Company Separation, contact Technical Support.

3 Configuring

3.1 Custom logo

A company can see a custom logo in the upper left of the user interface by adding a logo image to the relevant company record.

3.2 Bypassing Company Separation for a table

To bypass company separation for a table, find the table's record on the sys_dictionary table and add no_separation to the Attribute field. This will disable company separation for that table. Adding this attribute to an child table (e.g. "incident") will not effect the parent table (e.g., "task").

3.3 Bypassing Company Separation for a company

When the Company Separation plugin is installed, a "no_separation" true/false field is added to the core_company table. Any company with this field set to "true" will NOT be bound by company separation and will see records for all companies unless restricted in some other manner.

If you do not have the "no_separation" field on your core_company table, you do not have the Company Separation plugin properly installed.
