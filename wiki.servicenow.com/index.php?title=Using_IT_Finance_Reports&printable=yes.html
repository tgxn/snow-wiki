1 Overview

The Financial Management application provides several financial reports in overview and dashboard modules. These reports help you track where expenses are allocated and what kind of financial data is in the general ledger. In addition to the default reports, you can create custom reports that run on any table that the application uses. You can base reports on several components of the application, such as buckets, fiscal periods, and segments from the IT chart of accounts, including segments that you create.

The allocation data that the reports display are derived from allocations lines in the Cost Allocation [itfm_cost_allocation] table. Only the allocation lines marked final are included in reports.

You must have one of the Financial Management user roles can access the overview and dashboard modules.

Example of the Financial Overview showing a variety of financial reports

 Note: The overviews and dashboards contain a UI graphical element called Report Parameters that allows users to configure settings for the reports, such as the segments and fiscal period to display in the reports. Do not delete this widget.


1.1 Default Reports

The IT Finance application provides these reports that you can add to any homepage. The reports appear under IT Financial Management in the Add content window:

  • Account Overview: Reports for all accounts in a segment.
  • Business Service Overview: Reports specific to business services.
  • Business Unit Overview: Reports specific to business units.
  • CIO Dashboard: The dashboard that provides you with a quick summary of finances, operations, and projects.
  • Financial Overview: Reports showing allocations by segment, fiscal period, and buckets.
  • Trending Dashboard: Trend reports for segments.
IT Finance reports you can add to homepages

2 Using the Overview Modules

The Overview modules and Trending Dashboard are homepages. For details about editing gauges on homepages, see Adding Existing Gauges to a Homepage.

2.1 Using the Financial Overview

The Financial Overview module shows you a variety of reports, including total allocations, allocations by segment or fiscal period, and allocations for buckets and sub-buckets.

  1. Navigate to IT Finance > Overviews > Financial Overview.
  2. Under Report Parameters, select the segment, the account, and the fiscal period, up to five previous years.
  3. Click elements within the report to see a list of allocation lines. To open the list in another tab, press Ctrl for Windows or press Command for Macs when you click the element.

2.2 Using the Business Service Overview

The Business Service Overview module shows you reports for each business service. It also shows allocation data for buckets, business units, departments, projects, and servers that are related to the business service. You can also access the records for the projects and servers that have expenses allocated to them.

  1. Navigate to IT Finance > Overviews > Business Service Overview.
  2. Under Report Parameters, select the business service and the fiscal period for the previous five years or five quarters.
  3. Click elements within the report to see a list of allocation lines.

2.3 Using the Business Unit Overview

The business unit overview shows you reports for each of the business units based on the selected fiscal period. It also shows allocation data for servers, projects, departments, and business services that are related to the business unit. You can also access the records for the projects and servers that have expenses allocated to them.

  1. Navigate to IT Finance > Overviews > Business Unit Overview.
  2. Under Report Parameters, select the business unit and the fiscal period for the previous five years or five quarters.
  3. Click elements within the report to see a list of allocation lines.

2.4 Using the Account Overview

The account overview shows you allocation reports for accounts in any segment. Select a segment and an account first to view how allocations are made to other accounts that are related to the selected account. For example, if you select the HR account in the Department segment and there are two projects related to HR activities, the overview shows you how much is allocated to only those HR-related projects.

  1. Navigate to IT Finance > Overviews > Account Overview.
  2. Under Report Parameters, select the segment, account, and the fiscal period or the previous five years or five quarters.
  3. Click elements within the report to see a list of allocation lines.

3 Using the Dashboard Modules

A dashboard shows one report. Unlike the Overview modules, you cannot add or remove reports or change the layout of the page.

3.1 Using the Financial Dashboard

  1. Navigate to IT Finance > Dashboards > Financial Dashboard.
  2. Select a segment and fiscal period from the choice lists.
  3. Click elements within the report to see a list of allocation lines.
The dashboard showing allocations by business unit.

3.2 Using the Trending Dashboard

The trending dashboard shows allocation data for each segment and total allocations for all segments over time. Use the trending dashboard to understand how your expenses have increased or decreased throughout a range of fiscal periods.

  1. Navigate to IT Finance > Dashboards > Trending Dashboard.
  2. Select a fiscal period for the previous five years or five quarters.
  3. Click elements within the report to see a list of allocation lines.

4 Using Business Service Allocation Reports

Business Service forms display a bar chart report that shows the expenses allocated to that business service for each fiscal period during which there is an allocation.

  1. Navigate to Configuration > Business Services.
  2. Click the name of a business service.
  3. Right-click the form header and then select View > Finance.
    The graph appears at the bottom of the form. If it does not appear, configure the form to add it.

5 Using Project Management Allocation Reports

Project and portfolio forms display a pie chart report that shows the expenses allocated to that project or portfolio shown by the bucket the expense belonged to.

  1. Navigate to Project > Projects > All.
  2. Click the name of a project or portfolio you want to view.
  3. Right-click the form header and then select View > Finance.
    The graph appears at the bottom of the form. If it does not appear, configure the form to add it.

6 Using Vendor Allocation Reports

Company forms display a chart report that shows the expenses allocated to that company shown by fiscal period. Only companies with the Vendor check box selected can receive allocations.

  1. Navigate to Product Catalog > Catalog Definition > Vendors for vendors or User Administration > Companies.
  2. Click the name of a company.
  3. Right-click the form header to open the form context menu, and then select View > Finance.
    The graph appears at the bottom of the form. If it does not appear, configure the form to add it.

7 Generating Financial Reports

You can view and edit financial reports and generate new reports using the Reports application.

  1. Navigate to Reports > View/Run.
  2. To find the baseline IT Finance reports, search for reports using the tables in the application, such as Cost Allocation [itfm_cost_allocation] for allocated expense data or Groomed General Ledger Data [itfm_gl_data_groomed] for data in the general ledger.
  3. Click a report name, such as Expenses by Allocation State, to open it.
  4. Modify the report details, such as the Table or Type as needed.

See Reporting for more information about creating and editing reports.
