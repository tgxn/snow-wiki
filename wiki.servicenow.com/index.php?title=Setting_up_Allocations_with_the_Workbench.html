1 Overview

After you put expenses into buckets, set up allocations by assigning expenses to accounts and segments before moving on to the final stage, where you run the allocation engine.

The Allocation Setup stage

2 The Allocation Setup Page

On the Allocation Setup page, the sub-buckets that you created appear in the left pane, except for sub-buckets that you excluded from the cost model. The segments and their accounts appear in the middle pane, in the hierarchy that you defined in the data definition stage. The summary in the right pane provides a breakdown of how the expenses are assigned to accounts in each segment.

In the Allocation Setup stage, you can:

  • View bucket contents and split buckets into smaller buckets, if necessary.
  • Create and modify rollups to specify exactly how expenses are applied to higher-level segments in the hierarchy.
  • Assign expenses to accounts.
  • Review assigned expenses and revert bucket assignments if necessary.
The Allocation Setup page.

3 Viewing Bucket Contents

To modify what you see in the list of buckets:

  1. Click the gear icon () next to Allocation Setup.
  2. Show or hide the buckets that are already assigned by clicking the Show assigned buckets toggle. You can assign the expenses in buckets to accounts for unassigned buckets only.
  3. Sort the accounts by selecting Name or Amount in the Sort by list.
  4. Further sort the accounts by selecting Ascending or Descending from the Sort list.

View the records from the General Ledger Cleansed Data [itfm_gl_data_cleansed] table that belong to a bucket by clicking the information icon (). The records open in the list view in a new browser tab or browser window.

4 Splitting Buckets

Buckets contain groomed general ledger expenses. These expenses can be associated with a cost center, vendor, department, and location. You can split the expenses in a bucket by any of these attributes. For example, if a bucket includes expenses for two different vendors, you can split the bucket into two smaller buckets, one for each vendor. Each split bucket contains the groomed general ledger expense associated with that vendor.

The application saves split buckets in the ITFM Split Bucket [itfm_split_bucket] table, rather than the Bucket [itfm_bucket] table. Split buckets are accessible in the Allocation Setup stage only. You cannot go back to the Bucketing stage and put expenses in a split bucket.

To split buckets:

  1. Click the bucket tree icon () for the bucket you want to split.
    The split bucket window appears, showing you the amount of money in the bucket.
  2. From the Split by list, either select an attribute that is associated with the groomed general ledger expense or select Percentage.
    • If you select an attribute, the new buckets appear in the window with default names in the format [original bucket name] > [attribute name].
    • If you select Percentage, enter the percentages for the new buckets and give each bucket a name.
    A preview of the split buckets appear in the window along with the expense amounts for each bucket.
  3. Click Split Bucket.
  4. The application creates new buckets and splits the expenses in them accordingly by your settings. Buckets that are split appear with a folder icon.
    A split bucket

  5. To view a newly split bucket with the other buckets that were split along with it, click the folder icon.
    Splitting the Hardware bucket

To revert the bucket split, click Unsplit bucket.

5 Rollups for Allocations

A rollup is an allocation rule that specifies how a lower-level account in the segment hierarchy connects to other accounts above it in the hierarchy. This allows you to allocate expenses to the lower-level account and have expenses automatically allocated to the higher-level accounts.

Rollups can be account-specific or segment-specific. Account-specific rollups specify how expenses roll up from one specific account to one or more accounts above it. Segment-specific rollups specify how expenses in all the accounts for a segment roll up to all the accounts in the segment above it.

Rollups specified for an individual account takes precedence over the rollup specified for the segment. For example, if you create these two rollups:

  • An account-specific rollup that allocates expenses in the email account in the business service segment to the IT account in the business unit segment
  • A segment-specific rollup that allocates expenses in the business service segment to the business unit segment

The account-specific rollup allocates the expenses. The segment-specific rollup does not allocate any expenses.

5.1 Default Rollups for Segments and CIs

By default, the application creates rollups for segments in the hierarchy to link configuration items (CIs), such as servers and business services. These rollups take expenses assigned to a CI in a lower segment and roll them up to related CIs in the higher segment. The rollup that the application creates also includes a method and weighted metric. The metric uses the CI Relationship [cmdb_rel_ci] table to obtain the relationships between CIs in the segments.

For example, if a business service like Securities Lending uses a server named unix200, as defined in your CMDB, and you allocate expenses to that Unix server, the expenses roll up to Securities Lending. By default, the rollup between the lower segment with CIs and the higher segment with CIs is allocated equally. You can modify how the expenses roll up and change the relationship, if necessary.

Expenses rolling up from a unix server to a business service based on CI relationships.

5.2 Creating Rollups from One Account to Another

  1. Click the information icon () on an account. The account summary window appears, showing you:
    • The total amount of money currently assigned to this account.
    • The buckets that contribute expenses to this account.
    • The method that this account uses to roll up to the parent segments.
    • The accounts that roll up to this account.
    • The accounts that this account rolls up to.
    The E-Commerce account rolls 100% of expenses to the IT account

  2. Select a method from the Rollup Method choice list:
    • Segment's default method: Expenses follow the default rollup rule for CI relationships that was created during the Data Definition stage. This is the default option for items in the CMDB that roll up to other items in the CMDB.
    • Split by CMDB: Expenses that are assigned to an item in the CMDB roll up to related items as specified in the CMDB relationship. This option is available only when two CMDB items in a relationship are in the child and parent segment. For example, if a server is used by a business service and that used by relationship is already defined in the CMDB, the Split by CMDB option is available.
    • None: Expenses do not roll up to any parent accounts. This is the default option for items that are not in the CMDB that roll up to items that are in the CMDB.
    • Equal: Expenses roll up to the parent accounts by the same percentage.
    • Manual: Expenses roll up to the parent accounts by a percentage that you specify. Enter the amounts in the Percentage fields.
    • Weighted: Expenses roll up to parent accounts using a weighted metric that already exists. The metrics available are those that specify a rollup to the segment that the parent account belongs to. If a metric has the Enforce Relationship option selected, the segment that the child account belongs to is also considered. If a metric does not have the Enforce Relationship option selected, the segment of the child account is ignored.
    See Creating Allocation Metrics for more information on metrics.
  3. To link the account with an item above it, click Add Rollup.
  4. Select a segment item from the Parent account choice list.
  5. If you selected the manual rollup method, enter the amounts in the Percentage field.
  6. If you selected the weighted rollup method, select the metric that applies.
  7. Enter as many rollups as necessary. The percentages must add up to 100%.
  8. If you need to remove any of the accounts, click the delete icon ().
  9. When you are finished making all your changes, click Save Changes.
    The rollup rules appear in the right pane under the donut chart.
  10. To see only the accounts that a selected account rolls up to, click See relationships on the account.
    Only the linked accounts appear.
    Note: The color of the account that is assigned the expense is a randomly assigned color by the workbench. The color matches the account's color on the donut chart.
    An account rollup

  11. To show all accounts in the segment hierarchy, click Hide relationships.

When you create a rollup, the system automatically updates the system metric that is used to manage the segment relationships in the hierarchy. This metric is created by the application during the Data Definition stage. See Modifying the IT Chart of Accounts for more information about the metrics that are created at the Data Definition stage.

5.3 Creating Rollups from One Segment to Another

A segment can roll up to the segment above it in the hierarchy using a weighted metric.

  1. Click the information icon () on a segment. Only the root segment, which is at the top, does not have an information icon because it cannot roll up to any other segments. The segment summary window appears, showing you the rollup method selected, if any.
  2. Next to Rollup Method, select Weighted.
  3. Next to Divide using metric, select the metric.
    The metric automatically weights the amounts by the values in the Value column.
  4. To further change the amounts, modify the values in the Percentage column.
  5. Click Save Changes.
A rollup from the Project segment to the Business Service segment

6 Assigning Expenses

Setting up an allocation means assigning the expenses in a bucket to an account in a segment or to several accounts in a segment. The expenses in the bucket automatically roll up based on the allocation rules you created for rollups.

When you assign expenses, the application creates an allocation rule with the prefix BCK:ALLC: in the name and gives it an order value of 10 more than the highest value, not including the values for the rules created for rollups. Rules that the application creates for rollups have a default value of 1,000,000. The application also creates a method that specifies the percentage allocated to the account. For more information on allocation rules, see Creating Allocation Rules.

6.1 Assigning Expenses to an Account

  1. Drag a bucket to a specific account.
    Alternatively, do the following:
    1. Click the allocation icon () for the bucket you want to allocate.
    2. Under Allocate to, select a segment.
    3. Under Account, select the segment account to which you want to assign all expenses in the bucket.
    4. Click Go.
    The Create Allocation Rule window appears, showing you a summary of the allocation.
    The Allocation method is Allocate 100% by default. This means that all expenses in the bucket are allocated to the segment item that you dragged the bucket to.
  2. Click Create.
Creating a weighted allocation rule

6.2 Assigning Expenses to Several Accounts in a Segment

  1. Drag a bucket to the segment header to assign expenses to all accounts in the segment, or type text into the segment header to filter the accounts you want to assign expenses to.
    Alternatively, do the following:
    1. Click the allocation icon () for the bucket you want to allocate.
    2. Under Allocate to, select a segment.
    3. Leave the Account set to All Accounts.
    4. Click Go.
    The Create Allocation Rule window appears, showing you a summary of the allocation. If you filtered the segment by typing keywords into the segment header's search field, only the accounts that appear can be assigned expenses.
    Assigning expenses to Unix servers only

  2. Select an allocation method from the choice list:
    • Equal: Expenses are assigned to the accounts by the same percentage.
    • Manual: Expenses are assigned to the accounts by a specified percentage. Enter the amounts in the Percentage fields.
    • Weighted: Expenses are assigned to the accounts using a weighted metric.
  3. From the Divide among choice list, select a segment.
  4. If you selected a weighted method, select a preconfigured metric from the Divide using metric choice list. The only metrics that are available are those that specify the parent and child segments. See Creating Allocation Metrics for more information on metrics.
  5. Click Create.

7 Reviewing Expense Assignments

Review the expenses assigned to accounts and verify the information is correct in the Total Amount Allocated section in the right pane.

7.1 Reviewing Assigned Expenses

Click the information icon () on an account to view the allocation summary, which shows the following:

  • The total amount allocated.
  • The buckets that are assigned to the account. Click the information icon () next to the bucket to view the bucket summary.
  • The rollups to this account.
An account summary

7.2 Verifying the Total Amount Allocated

The donut chart on the right shows the assigned expenses for all segments. By default, the calculations in both the donut chart and the segment hierarchy include data from the new rules that you just created in the workbench, and any existing rules that you previously created. You can choose to turn off the preexisting rules, called User Rules, so that you see only the calculations from your newly created rules.

Verify the allocation information:

  • Select a segment from the Total Amount Assigned choice list to view the allocation for that segment. The breakdown for each account appears below the donut chart. To change whether or not the information in the donut chart is refreshed automatically when you make changes, click the gear icon () and click the Refresh Automatically toggle.
Assigned expenses for the Business Service segment.

  • View the allocation rules and rollups that you manually set up outside of the workbench by clicking the User Rules tab. On this tab you can:
    • Click a rule name to open the Cost Allocation Rule form for that rule.
    • Click the preview icon () to preview the allocation for that rule in the allocation viewer.
    • Click the Show check box to include allocation lines from existing, user-created rules in the calculations for assigned buckets. When you select this check box, the application includes existing allocation lines that are active on the accounts. The amounts that you see in the segments and in the donut chart are affected by the inclusion of these allocation lines. You should not select this option if you want the calculated values to show only what you assign from buckets.
    • Click See all rules to see a list of all allocation rules in the application.
The User Rules tab showing a rule created outside of the workbench.

7.3 Previewing Allocations in the Allocation Viewer

You can preview a graphical representation of how the workbench allocates the expenses in the bucket by opening the allocation viewer. To open the viewer, click the allocation preview icon () on the bucket. See Using the Allocation Viewer for details.

Previewing an allocation

7.4 Reverting Bucket Assignments

Reverting bucket assignments means taking the expenses out of the segments and accounts and leaving it in the bucket. Reverting buckets also deletes existing allocation lines for the working fiscal period that are associated with this bucket.

7.4.1 Reverting Bucket Assignments for a Single Bucket

To revert bucket assignments for a single bucket, click the information icon on a bucket to open the account summary, and then click Unallocate Bucket.

To revert bucket assignments for a single bucket and delete existing allocation lines for that working fiscal period, click the unassign bucket icon () on the bucket, and then click Yes on the confirmation window. You can also delete existing allocation lines for all fiscal periods by clicking click here on the confirmation window, and then clicking Delete.

Deleting hardware capital expenses

7.4.2 Reverting All Bucket Assignments

To revert all bucket assignments:

  1. Click the gear icon () next to Allocation Setup.
  2. Click Unassign All Buckets.
  3. Confirm the action.

You can also revert expenses outside the workbench. See Reverting Allocations for more information.

8 Next Step

When you are finished setting up allocations, click the Review tab to go to the final stage.
