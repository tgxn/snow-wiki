1 Creating a Rota

Administrators can create a rota for users of the instance and define new schedules specifically for the roster. For more information, see Using Schedules.

  1. Navigate to On-Call Rotation > Create New Rota.
  2. Select the group to be scheduled by the new rota.
  3. [Optional] Give the rota a name.
  4. Select a start date.
  5. Select a schedule type. This associates a roster (who) with a schedule type (when).

Each rota is associated with a schedule. Rosters in a rota will automatically populate according to the schedule on the rota and the rotation internal of the roster. e.g., if your rota has a schedule of "Workday 8-5", and its roster has a rotation interval of "Weekly", members of the roster will be scheduled a week at a time, on call Monday through Friday from 8-5.

Two rotas for the same group cannot have schedule spans that overlap. e.g., if you already have a rota for the Network group with a schedule of "Workdays 8-5", you cannot create a new Network rota with a schedule of "24/7" since it would overlap the existing rota's schedule span.

Note: The on-call scheduling feature replaces group on-call rotation starting with the Eureka release.


1.1 Manually Defined Rotas

If you specify that your rota is a Manual Schedule, ServiceNow does not automatically populate its rosters and schedule the group members. You have the option to do that manually.

  1. Navigate to On-Call Rotation > My Groups Rotas.
  2. Locate/modify the rota you just added.
  3. The default filter on this module specifies the groups of which you are a member.

Manually populated rotas must define active periods before coverage can be defined. Coverage is required within the active periods.

2 Rota Reminder

The Send On-Call Reminders and Reminder Lead Time (days) sends a notification to the person on call a specified number of days before their on-call obligation.

3 Rosters

You can access rosters for a specific rota in a related list on the Rota form. A roster defines a subset of group members who will participate in an on-call roster. You cannot add members to a roster who are not in the group. Initially, roster members are automatically populated from the Group but you can remove users that will not participate in a rotation by clicking the “edit” button on the related list of roster members.

Note: Only users who are in a group with the role rota_manager can edit the related list, or users who have the rota_admin role.


If a roster's rotation should begin at a particular time of day (perhaps on-call responsibility transfers at 8am instead of midnight), uncheck “All day rotation” and you will be able to specify a start time.

Notification rules may also be specified on the roster to notify roster members when a task is assigned to their group.

4 Rota Notification Rules

A rota can have one or more notification rules defined for it, to notify group members or a notification device when a task is assigned to their group but has not yet been assigned to an individual. Notifications cease once the task is assigned to an individual. You define who to notify, how many times, and when. You can define notification rules in a related list on the rota form.

The property com.snc.on_call_rotation.reminders.showtz controls if the user's timezone is included in on-call rotation notifications containing time information.

A default Notification Rule is defined with one notification and no repeats. Also, instead of receiving separate emails, users will receive one email with all of the up-coming on-call duties.

4.1 How to Enable On-Call Notifications

To enable on-call notifications so that your rota notification rules have any effect, the following business rules must be activated:

  • On-call Rotation Notify - sets up the notification rules and escalations
  • On-call Rotation Cancel - cancels the notification escalations

4.2 Who

There are four options:

  • Rotate through members - specify the roster and how many members to be notified in turn
  • Group device - email address, pager, etc.
  • Member - a specific group member
  • On-call only - only the group member who is on-call when the notification is generated. This option is only valid for manually populated rotas (those without a roster).
 Note: If you only want to send a notification to the roster member who is currently on-call, select Rotate through members and set the # of members to notify to 1.


4.3 When

Three fields here:

  • Initial delay (minutes) - an optional delay before notifications start. This value is added to the base start time of Escalations. For example, suppose the escalation conditions are met at 15:00:00 and the initial delay field is set to 15 minutes. ServiceNow sends out the first notification for the roster at 15:15:00. If there are multiple rosters, each starts at the computed time equal to <base start time> + <Roster.Initial Delay>.
  • # attempts - the number of times ServiceNow notifies each person or device. A blank value causes ServiceNow to notify all roster members in order.
  • Subsequent delay (minutes) - if you are notifying multiple members of a roster, this is where you can specify, for example, that the 2nd person will be notified 15 minutes after the 1st, the 3rd person 30 minutes after that, and the 4th person 45 minutes later
