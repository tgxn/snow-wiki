1 Overview

Live feed allows users to post and share content with other users in a ServiceNow instance (requires the Live Feed plugin). Use live feed to:

  • Post and reply to messages.
  • Upload and share attachments and links.
  • Rate messages.
  • Filter, sort, and search messages.
  • Tag messages and search or filter by tags.
  • Receive email notifications when new messages are posted.
  • Create and join groups for focused discussions (see Using Groups in Live Feed).
  • Collaborate on important records like incidents, problems, and changes.
 Note: The live feed functionality changed significantly with live feed v2 (available with the Fuji release). This page describes live feed v1. For information about live feed v2, see Live Feed.


2 Updating Your Profile

Your profile identifies your contributions to conversations. It is created automatically the first time you use live feed and consists of:

  • Name: from your user account in ServiceNow. Your name appears on your messages and on messages you rate (see Rating Content).
  • Picture: select any picture. Your picture appears next to your messages and at the top of your feed (see Filtering by Feed).

To update your profile picture:

  1. Navigate to Social IT > Live Feed.
  2. Click your picture in the top of the content frame.
  3. Browse to the desired picture file and click Open.
 Note: Changes to your profile picture affect both chat and live feed (if the Chat plugin is activated).

3 Posting and Sharing Content

3.1 Posting Messages

Post a message to open a new topic of conversation. To participate in an existing conversation, see Replying to Messages.

To post a new message:

  1. Navigate to Social IT > Live Feed.
  2. Enter a message in the What are you working on? field. Use tags, if desired.
  3. Add attachments and links if desired.
  4. Click Update.
Post a message

3.2 Replying to Messages

Reply to messages to participate in an existing topic of conversation. To open a new topic of conversation, see Posting Messages.

To reply to a message or to a reply:

  1. Navigate to Social IT > Live Feed.
  2. Locate the message to reply to.
  3. Click Reply under the message text.
  4. Enter a message in the Write a reply field. Use tags, if desired.
  5. Add attachments and links if desired.
  6. Click Reply.
Post a reply

3.3 Deleting Messages

To delete any of your own posts or replies, click Delete under the message text. The message disappears from the feed.

3.4 Attaching Files and Links to Messages

Share content with other users by adding image attachments and links to messages. You can also attach any ServiceNow supported file type to live feed (Calgary release). Administrators can manage attachment file settings, such as supported file types and maximum attachment file size, by editing system properties.

To attach files and links to a post or reply:

  1. Begin composing a post or reply (see Posting Messages or Replying to Messages).
  2. For each item to be attached:
    1. Click the Image upload () icon or the Attachment () icon (Calgary release).
    2. Click Choose Files, browse to the desired file, and click Attach.
    3. Click Add Another Attachment to add more files.
    Adding attachments

  3. For each link to be attached:
    1. Click the Add a link button.
    2. Enter the link URL and Link text.
    3. Click OK.
  4. Click Update or Reply.
Adding attachments and links to the Company Feed

 Note: To remove all files or links, click the X in the upper right corner of the files or links section before posting your message.


3.5 Rating Content

You can rate content by indicating that you like a message. To like a message, click Like under the message text. Your name is added to the Liked by bar beneath the message.

Like a message

4 Viewing Content

4.1 Filtering by Feed

A feed is a stream of related messages. You can view the following feeds:

  • Company Feed: the main feed that contains all posts except those posted to private or unlisted groups.
    Navigate to Social IT > Live Feed and click the Company Feed () button.
  • User feeds: filter messages to show only the conversations a specific user has contributed to.
    Click the user name or profile picture next to a post to view the feed for that user. To view your own feed, click your name in the top left of the content frame (under Welcome).
  • Tag feeds: filter messages to show only the conversations where at least one of the messages contains the tag.
    Click the tag name under the message, in the list of available tags, or in the feed navigator.
  • Group feeds: filter messages to show only the conversations that are posted to a specific group.
    Click the group name on a message or in the list of groups. To learn more, see Using Groups in Live Feed.
  • My Feed: filter messages to show the conversations that:
    • Are posted to groups of which you are a member.
    • Are posted by a user that you follow.
    • Contain a tag that you follow.
    Click My Feed (). To learn more, see My Feed.

4.2 Sorting

To sort the messages in a feed:

  1. Click Options ().
  2. Select the desired sort order:
    • Order by Created: the most recent new post appears at the top of the feed.
    • Order by Last Activity: the most recent new post or post with the most recent reply appears at the top of the feed.
 Note: Message time stamps are indicated relative to the current time (for example, 1 minute ago). To see the exact post time, point to the time stamp.

4.3 Searching

To search all messages, enter a term in the search field and then press Enter or click the Search button (). Use tags, if desired.

4.4 Viewing Older Messages

When first opening a feed, only recent messages are displayed (according to the sort order, see Sorting).

To view older messages, scroll to the bottom of the feed and click More. The next set of messages appears.

5 Tags

Tags are words marked with a hash (#) symbol in messages. Tags are a way to categorize messages by keyword or topic for improved search results. For example, to identify messages associated with VPN questions, add the tag #VPN in the message text.

5.1 Tagging Messages

To tag a keyword or topic in a message, enter a # symbol before the word. The tag is added to the tag bar beneath the message and all feed users can search and filter by the tag to find the message.

Tips for using tags effectively include:

  • Limit the number of tags per message (no more than 3 is a good guideline).
  • Use tags only in messages that are relevant to the tag topic.
  • Write a message first, and then add tags only if they add value. Answer the question, "What are you working on?", rather than "What tags apply to what you are working on?"
  • Use camel case (remove the space and use an uppercase letter to start each word) to create a tag that is more than one word long. For example, to create a topic about the service desk, use #ServiceDesk.
Message containing the #VPN tag

5.2 Viewing Available Tags

To view a list of available tags:

  1. Click Tags ().
  2. Select the list of tags to view:
    • Popular: shows only the most popular tags.
    • Recent: shows only the most recent tags.
    • Show all: shows all available tags.
 Note: Administrators can configure the maximum number of popular and recent tags to display. To modify these properties, navigate to Social IT > Feed Administration > Properties and enter the values in the following fields:
  • The maximum number of popular tags displayed (glide.live_feed.max_popular_tags)
  • The number of recent tags to show in the tag display fields (glide.live_feed.max_recent_tags)


5.3 Changing Tag Names

Over time, users may create many tags with similar names (for example, competitive, competitor, and competition). To combine tags for better searching and filtering, change the names of similar tags to a standard name. Administrators can restrict who can rename tags.

To change the name of a tag:

  1. Expand the feed navigator or view the list of available tags ().
  2. Double-click in the empty area beside the tag name. The tag name becomes highlighted.
  3. Enter a new tag name.
  4. Press Enter. The new tag name replaces the old tag name on the tag list and in the text of any messages that include the tag.
Change Tag Name

5.4 Searching and Filtering by Tag

To search for conversations that contain a tag, enter the tag in the search field and then press Enter or click the Search button (). For example, to search for messages with the #VPN tag, enter #VPN.

To filter the feed by a tag (show only conversations where at least one of the messages contains the tag), click the tag name in any of the following places:

  • The tag bar under the message text.
  • The list of available tags ().
  • The feed navigator.

5.5 Using the Feed Navigator

The feed navigator allows users to view and filter by tags in one click. Administrators must activate the module Live Feed with Layout to provide access to the feed navigator.

To use the feed navigator, navigate to Social IT > Live Feed with Layout.

  • To view the tag list, point to the feed navigator bar next to the application navigator.
  • To keep the tag list expanded, click the Expand button (). To collapse an expanded tag list, click the Collapse button ().
  • To filter by a tag on the list, click the tag name.
Feed Navigator

6 My Feed

My Feed lets users customize live feed to view the content that is most important to them. This feature is enabled by default. Administrators can disable My Feed by navigating to Social IT > Properties and changing the Enables user access to My Feed capabilities property (glide.live_feed.my_feed_enabled).

With My Feed, users can:

  • Follow user feeds and tags.
  • View messages that are most interesting to you in one place.
  • Manage what you are following and which groups you belong to.
  • Post messages to any group you belong to.


To view your My Feed:

  1. Navigate to Social IT > Live Feed.
  2. Click My Feed.

6.1 Following User Feeds and Tags

When you follow user feeds or tags, you are choosing to see the associated messages in your My Feed. You will see only the messages you have access to; you will not see messages that are posted to private or unlisted groups unless you are a member.

To follow a user feed (the conversations a specific user has contributed to), click the Follow button under the user's profile picture beside a post.

Follow a user feed

To follow a tag (the conversations where at least one of the messages contains the tag):

  1. View the tag feed by clicking the tag name under the message, in the list of available tags, or in the feed navigator.
  2. Click the Follow button at the top of the tag feed.
Follow a tag

To stop following a user feed or tag, use My Selections. To learn more, see Configuring My Feed.

6.2 Configuring My Feed

To manage what you are following and which groups you belong to:

  1. Navigate to Social IT > Live Feed.
  2. Click My Feed.
  3. Click Configure My Feed.
  4. Select a tab for preferences to manage:
    • Groups: displays information for the groups you belong to. To leave a group, click Leave beside the group information.
    • Users: displays information for the users whose feeds you are following. To stop following a user feed, click Unfollow beside the user information.
    • Tags: displays information for the tags you are following. To stop following a tag, click Unfollow beside the tag information.
My Feed selections


 Note: If you leave a group that is associated with a record, such as an incident, you will automatically rejoin that group if you view the record again (Calgary release).


6.3 Posting Messages from My Feed

You can post a new message to any group you belong to or to the company feed from your My Feed.

To post a message from My Feed:

  1. Open your My Feed.
  2. Compose your message and add attachments and links. To learn more, see Posting Messages.
  3. In the To list, select the group where you want to post the message.
  4. Click Update.
    Select a group

7 Subscribing to Email Notifications

To receive an email notification whenever a message is posted:

  1. Navigate to Self-Service > My Profile.
  2. Click the Notification Preferences related link.
  3. Click the green plus sign to add a new subscription.
  4. In the Notification Message field, select Live Message All Subscription.
  5. [Optional] Define filters and schedules for the notifications (see Using Subscription Based Notifications).
  6. Click Submit.
Notification Preferences

8 Using Live Feed with UI11

To use live feed with UI11 (requires the UI11 plugin):

  1. If do not see a gray toolbar on the left side of the screen (called your edge), click the Switch to the new UI link in the banner.
  2. Navigate to Social IT > Live Feed.
  3. Drag the Live Feed module to the edge.
  4. Point to the bookmark on your edge. When the tip window appears, click Edit Bookmark.
  5. Select the Flyout check box.
  6. Click Update.
    • You can now access live feed in a flyout window without navigating away from your working panes.
    • Notifications appear on the edge when new messages are posted in your My Feed.
    Live feed bookmark with notification of new messages

    • When you open the bookmark, a blue circle appears beside new messages.
    Live feed in a flyout window
