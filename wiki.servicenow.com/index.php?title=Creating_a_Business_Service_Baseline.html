1 Overview

The Business Service baseline feature helps you to understand and control the changes that are made to your business services.

2 Business Service Baseline

A business service baseline is a snapshot of the state of a business service that captures the current state of the CIs and relationships that make up that service. When you define a baseline for a service, you define a baseline for all the CIs and relationships that are part of that service.

You can baseline services that have been created by the ServiceWatch module. (These services are tracked in the svc_ci_assoc and svc_rel_assoc tables.) Each business service has its own individual baseline. You cannot baseline a group of services.

A business service baseline is automatically updated to include any changes made to that service from the approved change process, such as with the proposed changes feature.

You can create an exclusion list for the service baseline feature. The exclusion list allows you to exclude certain properties of a CI from the baseline. If an item in the exclusion list is modified, the service is not marked dirty. An example of when this may be useful is for the temperature property of a system. The temperature can change often, and you may not want to know each time.

The service is marked dirty if either of the following is true:

  • If any of the elements that are a part of the service have been modified
  • If any elements have been added or removed from the service

The service is marked clean when all elements that make up the service are in the same state as the baseline.

3 View the Service Baseline Dashboard

To view the Service Baseline Dashboard:

  1. Request activation of the Service Model plugin.
  2. Navigate to Configuration > Baselines > Service Baseline

The Service Baseline Dashboard lists all business services in the left pane. The business services display in the following order:

  • Dirty: Services that have been changed from their baseline, in alphabetical order.
  • Clean: Services that have a baseline set, in alphabetical order.
  • Not Baselined: Services that have not had a baseline set, in alphabetical order.

4 Service Baseline Search Options

To search for a service:

  • Enter text into the Search box in the Service List pane (left pane) of the Service Baseline Dashboard.

To search within a service:

  • Enter text into the Search box in the Association List pane (right pane) of the Service Baseline Dashboard.
    • You can search all assocation properties, which includes name, class name, CI, relationship, sys_id, changed property name, old value of property, new value of property, relationship type.
    • You can limit the search to the name of a class using the Class option in the drop-down menu.

5 View the Details of a Service Baseline

To view the details of a business service:

  1. Navigate to Configuration > Baselines > Service Baseline.
  2. Click a Business Service.

Details of the service that is selected in the left pane displays in the right pane. The right pane displays elements in the following order:

  • CIs or Relationships that have been added to the service since the baseline.
  • CIs or Relationships that have been removed from the service since the baseline.
  • CIs or Relationships that have changed since the baseline.
  • Cis or Relationships that are unchanged from the baseline.

The following notations display on this page:

The service has a baseline defined, and no changes have been made to the baseline outside of the official approval process.
The service has a baseline, and changes have been made to the baseline outside of the official approval process.
The CI or relationship is the same as the defined baseline.
The service, CI or relationship does not have baseline defined.
The CI or relationship of the service has been modified from the defined baseline. Details of the change display underneath the icon.
The CI or relationship has been added to the service.
The CI or relationship has been removed from the service.
If the CI is part of multiple business services that are baselined, the other services are listed.

You can perform the following actions in the Service Baseline Details form:

Task Action
Filter the list of services in the left navigation Select one of the following options:
  • All: Displays all the services.
  • Dirty: Displays only services that have been modified from the baseline.
  • Clean: Displays only services that are the same as the baseline.
  • Not baselined: Displays only services that do not have a baseline defined.
Filter the contents that display in the Service Details form. Enter text into the Search box. You can search on any text that displays on the form. For example, to display only relationships, enter ::.
Group the service details form by the type of the change from the baseline Use the default Change Type option in the drop-down menu.
Group the service details form by the class of the CI Use the Class option in the drop-down menu.

6 Create an Initial Baseline for a Service

When you create a baseline for a business service, the service is monitored. The Service Baseline Dashboard displays when there are changes to the CIs or the relationships that make up the service.

To create an initial baseline for a business service:

  1. Install the Service Model plugin.
  2. Navigate to Configuration > Baselines > Service Baseline
  3. Click a business service from the dashboard.
  4. Click the Create button.

7 Manage the Baseline for a Business Service

When changes have been made to a CI or relationship, you must approve the changes to add them to the baseline.

To approve the changes:

  1. Click the individual line items to approve, or click the top square to select all line items.
  2. Click the Update button.

You can remove the baseline for a business service, which will stop tracking modifications.

To delete the baseline:

  1. Click the business service to delete in the Service List.
    A Delete Alert window displays.
  2. Click the Delete button.

7.1 Manage the Baseline When a CI is Part of Multiple Business Services

When a CI is part of more than one business service, and the CI has been modified, all business services that contain the CI and are baselined are marked to the Dirty state.

If you click the Update button for one of the business services, you receive the following message to warn you that the CI is part of other business services.


In this situation, you have the following options:

Use this option... For this result...
Exclude This option provides a partial update of the baseline. The baseline definition is updated with all changes except the CI that is part of multiple business services. This option allows you to do more research to determine how the update of the CI will affect the other business services. When you use this option, all business services that use this CI remain in the dirty state.
OK This option updates the baseline, including the the CI that is part of multiple business services. After you use this option, the current business service is in the clean state. Other business services that contain this CI may also be marked in the clean state, if this CI was the only remaining modification to the other service. Do not use this option unless you are certain of the impact to the other business services.
Cancel This option cancels the baseline update and returns to the Business Service Details page.

8 Creating a Business Service Baseline Exclusion

You can create an exclusion list for the service baseline feature. The exclusion list allows you to exclude certain properties of a CI from the baseline. If an item in the exclusion list is modified, the service is not marked 'dirty. For example, consider the temperature property of a system. The temperature can change often and you may not want to know each time it does.

To create a service baseline exclusion:

  1. Navigate to the CI record with the property that you want to exclude.
    Configuration > Business Services
  2. Right-click on the field that you want to exclude.
  3. Select the Show menu item
    In the following example, the field name is assigned_to.
  4. In the popup that displays, note the table name in the title. The field name is listed as the Element.
    In the following example, the table name is cmdb_ci_computer.
  5. Navigate to Configuration > Baselines > Service Baseline Exclusion Table.
  6. Click New.
  7. Fill in the form using the following information:
    Field Name: Enter the label name.
    Table Name: Enter the table name.
  8. Click Submit.
