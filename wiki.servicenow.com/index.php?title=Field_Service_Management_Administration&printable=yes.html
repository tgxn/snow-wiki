1 Overview

Administrators can create, assign, and configure different modules in Field Service Management including skills, territories, customers, and service orders.

Field Service Management pages are enabled with multiple views. Views are set by navigating to list pages, such as "Awaiting Qualification," and right-clicking on header bar to choose the appropriate view.



Note: In the Calgary release, Field Service Management is deprecated and replaced with the new Work Management application.


1.1 Overview Module

The Overview module enables administrators to create and display various field service management-related gauges. For information on creating and adding gauges, see Adding Gauges.

To open the Field Service Management Overview module, navigate to Field Service Management > Overview.

2 Service Order

For information about service orders, including part requirements and asset usage, see Using Field Service Management.

3 Sourcing and Transfer Orders

Sourcing parts is the process of finding the parts needed to execute a service order. Parts are stored in stockrooms. Transfer orders track the movement of parts between stockrooms and handover of parts to the field service agent assigned to the service order. For more information, see Part Requirement Sourcing and Transfer Orders.

4 Inventory Management

Satisfactory execution of service orders normally requires installation or exchange of parts on the configuration item being serviced. Use the inventory management feature to coordinate transfer and delivery to the field service agent. ServiceNow tracks current part location (such as stockroom) and status (such as reserved), as well as movement of parts between different stockrooms.

4.1 Field Service Agent (FSA) Stockroom

Field Service Management creates a new many-to-many relationship between FSAs and stockrooms. This relationship does the following:

  • Determines part delivery location after the FSA is assigned to the service order.
  • Links the stockroom to an FSA.
  • Manages the delivery of a part to the FSA.
 Note: Every time the fsm_agent role is assigned to a user, a personal Field Agent stockroom (priority 2) is automatically created for that user. When a part is assigned the location “Field Agent Stockroom," ServiceNow records that part as having been delivered. This allows the transfer order to be closed. The personal stockroom is deleted as soon as the role is withdrawn from the user.


For information about determining what assets are in stock, see Viewing a List of Assets in Stock.

For information about the stockrooms, see Stockrooms.

4.2 Creating and Editing Field Service Agents

Field service agents are users with the fsm_agent or fsm_admin role. For more information about field service management roles, see User Roles.

To create a new field service agent:

  1. Navigate to Field Service Management > Inventory Management > Agents.
  2. Click New.
    The User form is displayed in Field Services view. The fsm_agent role is automatically added to the Roles field.
  3. Add information to the form.
  4. Click Submit.

To view and edit information about a specific field service agent:

  1. Navigate to Field Service Management > Inventory Management > Agents.
  2. Click on a User ID.
  3. View and edit information as necessary.

5 Skills Management

Use Skills Management to create skills and assign them to users or groups. Skills can be, for example, knowledge of:

  • a category of products, such as printers, hardware, or mobile devices
  • products from a specific company, such as Microsoft or IBM

Create skills based on the type of service orders field service agents will have to complete and the knowledge they need to execute service orders successfully.

For information about creating users and groups, see Creating Users and Associating to a Group.

5.1 Creating Skills

To create a skill:

  1. Navigate to Field Service Management > Skills Management > Skills.
  2. Click New.
  3. Give the skill a unique, descriptive Name.
  4. (Optional) Add a Description of the skill.
  5. Click Submit.

5.2 Adding Contained Skills to a Skill

To add a contained skill to a skill:

  1. Navigate to Field Service Management > Skills Management > Skills.
  2. Click on a skill Name.
  3. In the Contains Skills related list, click Edit.
  4. Double-click on a skill in the Collection list to add it to the Contains Skills List.
  5. When you are finished adding contained skills, click Save.

5.3 Assigning Skills to Users

To assign skills to users:

  1. Navigate to Field Service Management > Skills Management > Users.
  2. Open a user record by clicking a User ID.
  3. In the Skills related list, click Edit.
  4. Double-click on a skill in the Collection list to add it to the Skills List.
  5. When you are finished adding skills, click Save.

If you assign a given skill to the user and the skill contains other skills, the contained skills are assigned to the user automatically.

5.4 Assigning Skills to Groups

To assign skills to groups:

  1. Navigate to Field Service Management > Skills Management > Groups.
  2. Open the appropriate group record.
  3. In the Skills related list, click Edit.
  4. Double-click on a skill in the Collection list to add it to the Skills List.
  5. When you are finished adding skills, click Save.

If you assign a given skill to the group and this skill contains other skills, the other skills are assigned automatically.

6 Territory Management

The Territory Management application enables you to assign a single location or multiple locations to a user or group. For example:

  • a field service agent (user) is assigned to the location Pasadena
  • multiple field service agents (group) are assigned to the location Southern California

6.1 Creating Locations

To create a location:

  1. Navigate to Field Service Management > Territory Management > Locations.
  2. Click New.
  3. Type in a Name.
  4. Add information to the fields as appropriate.
  5. Click Submit.

6.2 Adding a Location To Another Location

You can assign a parent to a location to create a hierarchy of locations. For example, open the record for Brooklyn and assign it the parent New York.

To add a parent location to a location:

  1. Navigate to Field Service Management > Territory Management > Locations.
  2. Click a location Name.
  3. In the Parent field, add a location.
  4. Click Update.

6.3 Assigning Location to Users

Territory management at the user level is not used for automations.

To assign a location to a user:

  1. Navigate to Field Service Management > Territory Management > Users.
  2. Open a user record by clicking a User ID.
  3. In the Field Services Locations Covered related list, click Edit.
  4. Double-click on a location in the Collection list to add it to the Field Services Locations Covered list.
  5. Click Save.

6.4 Assigning Location to Group

The system checks the location hierarchy to determine which group is assigned to the location. The goal is to assign a group that is as close as possible to the location where the work must be done.

To assign a location to a group:

  1. Navigate to Field Service Management > Territory Management > Groups.
  2. Open a group record by clicking a Name.
  3. In the Field Services Locations Covered related list, click Edit.
  4. Double-click on a location in the Collection list to add it to the Field Services Locations Covered list.
  5. Click Save.

7 Customer Management

7.1 Customers

The Field Services team provides services to internal or external customers. ServiceNow Field Service Management uses the Customer view of the Company form. This view enables you to:

  • Link new service orders to companies being qualified as customers.
  • Associate a service contract with a customer who receives services.

To create a new customer:

  1. Navigate to Field Service Management > Customer Management > Customers.
  2. Click New.
  3. Fill in the form.
  4. Click Submit.

To view an existing customer:

  1. Navigate to Field Service Management > Customer Management > Customers.
  2. Click the customer Name.

Customer view displays all customer information, including Users, Locations, and Configuration Items.


7.2 Service Contracts

The service contracts option provides a link between the customer, the contract, and the SLAs associated with the service order.

In the base system, the Service Contract table stores information about service contracts for asset management purposes and extends the Contract table. Activating the SLA Contract Add-on plugin adds relations to this table to make it useful for Field Service Management purposes. Field Service Management also links a service contract to a customer company. The Service Contract record contains related lists for:

  • SLAs
  • Contract CIs
  • Contract Locations
  • Contract Groups
  • Contract Users
  • Child Contracts

For information about how to define a service contract, see Defining a Service Contract.

In Field Service Management, the information on Vendor is replaced by information on Customer. For everything else related to the service contract definition and SLA definition, see SLA Contract Add-on Plugin.
