1 Overview

At ServiceNow, it is our goal and duty to maintain the quality of Wiki content to the highest possible standards so that everyone can get the most out of the software platform. We believe that reader feedback is a key component of true quality and effectiveness, and we encourage all readers to use our ratings and feedback tools.

We enact this Code of Conduct for reader feedback on the ServiceNow Wiki. Follow these basic guidelines to help us build the best experience for everyone.

2 Do

  • Post comments or questions about page content by using the Feedback tab.
  • Be specific about your question or feedback. Answer follow up questions, if necessary.
  • Add pages to your watchlist and sign up for email notifications so that you know when someone responds or updates the page.
  • Help others when you can.
  • Maintain professionalism and exercise common courtesy toward all users.

3 Don't

To benefit all users, don't send, post, or distribute:

  • Links to commercial services or inappropriate external websites.
  • Spam (multiple copies in one area or the same content in multiple areas).
  • Any comment that is intended to harass, belittle, humiliate, threaten, abuse, or cause embarrassment to a fellow user.
  • Material that contains vulgar, obscene, or indecent language or images.
  • Material that is bigoted, hateful, or racially offensive.
  • Material that advocates or references illegal activity.
  • Unauthorized copyrighted material.

4 Violations

These guidelines are intended to foster information sharing and create a comfortable work environment for all wiki users. ServiceNow Wiki administrators, at their sole discretion, will delete any content that violates the code of conduct either directly or in its intent. While we may attempt to notify contributors if we move or delete their content, we are under no obligation to do so. Depending on the nature of the violation, we may also restrict or terminate a user's account.

We try to monitor all communications, but we may miss something. If you read or receive any inappropriate or threatening communication from another wiki user, please don't hesitate to contact ServiceNow. Let's work together to build a great experience for everyone!
